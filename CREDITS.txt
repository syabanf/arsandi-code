ARSANDI CODE — Asset Credits
============================

Hero sprites — Saka, Kara, Zell (player party)
-----------------------------------------------
Project-owned. AI-generated by the project author and authored as the canonical
party hero sheets. Source sheets in Assets/heroes/{saka,kara,zell}.png;
sliced into per-state strips (idle, walk, front, back, …) by tools/heroes.mjs.

Townsfolk / NPC archetypes (merchant, elder, villager, guard)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Built from the Universal LPC Spritesheet (Liberated Pixel Cup) assets.
Licensed under CC-BY-SA 3.0 and GPL 3.0.

Contributors include (non-exhaustive): Stephen Challener (Redshrike),
Johannes Sjölund (wulax), Marcel van de Steeg (MadMarcel), Daniel Eddeland,
Lanea Zimmerman (Sharm), Manuel Riecke (MrBeast), and the LPC community.

Source / generator: https://github.com/jrconway3/Universal-LPC-spritesheet

As required by CC-BY-SA 3.0, these derived sprite sheets and any further
modifications are distributed under the same license (CC-BY-SA 3.0).

Organic monster sprites (serpents, slime, eyeball, plant-maw, wraiths)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The 11 organic-enemy sprites — data-eel, bog-leviath, digital-jelly,
code-squid, dream-stalker, packet-wisp, frost-wisp, memory-shade,
data-wraith, tide-wraith, regret-wisp — are derived from the
"[LPC] Monsters" pack by Charles Sanchez (CharlesGabriel), bagzie,
and bluecarrot16. Licensed under CC-BY-SA 3.0+ and GPL 3.0+ (the bat
sprite, when used, is additionally OGA-BY 3.0 — not used in this build).

Source: https://opengameart.org/content/lpc-monsters
Composited per-enemy via tools/monsters-lpc.mjs (front-facing frames
extracted, hue-rotated for elemental palette swaps, packed into 2-frame
idle sheets).

As required by CC-BY-SA 3.0, these derived sprites are distributed under
the same license (CC-BY-SA 3.0).

Procedural art (tiles, machine enemies, props, UI, FX)
------------------------------------------------------
Original, generated by this project's tooling (tools/minions.mjs,
tools/build-world.mjs, runtime canvas). All drones, sentries, husks,
constructs, sentinels, and machine-type enemies remain procedural —
they fit the game's techno-fantasy theme better than any fantasy pack.
Owned by the project.
